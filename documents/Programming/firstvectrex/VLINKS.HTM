    Vectrex developing related links

  • An archive of all things Vectrex

      Following is an extract of the readme-file (hope you don't mind me putting it here...) 
      This is the archive of all things Vectrex

The Vectrex was/is a video game system, circa 1982. It is unique in
that it contains a built-in, black&white vector display CRT (like a
TV, only it draws point-to-point lines under program control). By
today's standards, it's no match for the full color, full sound,
blood and guts video systems. But the Vectrex has a loyal following
that appreciates the machine for how much the designers could squeeze
out of a 68A09 and 2k of RAM. vectrex newbies are referred to the FAQ
for more details...

The archive contains lots of information, including binaries and hex
dumps of all known released games (the copyright holder has granted
his permission for the personal use of this software!), copies of the
instruction manuals, scans of the color screen overlays, and lots of
technical insight.
        ftp://ftp.csus.edu/pub/vectrex/

      Following is a list of subdirectories:

        darktower/ stuff related to darktower, rom-images and instructions
        devsys/ Vectrex developers System 95, how to connect a vectrex<->PC
        disassembled/ some dissassembled Vectrex roms, done by Fred Taft
        emulator/ Keith's emulator
        fixed/ some 'broken' roms were fixed... look for them here
        goggles/ information regarding vectrex 3d goggles (pictures and text)
        images/ rom images of various vectrex roms (nearly all of them)
        instructions/ instructions for many (but not all) vectrex roms
        interface/ another vectrex<->pc interface
        manual/ vectrex service manual in TIF format (page 2-7 missing)
        overlays/ many if not all overlays scanned in JPG or GIF format
        pcjohn/ PC Johns two new vectrex roms (third one coming some day?)
        pictures/ various vectrex pictures...
        rec.games.vectrex/ collection of newsgroup articles from 1994-1996
        text/ collection of various texts about vectrex

  • DVE V1.20 related

      Scitech! Those guys are doing a great job writing their VESA 2.0 compliant driver: Display Doctor. Get the newest driver at the homepage.
        http://www.scitechsoft.com
      Furthermore, to compile version 1.20 and higher you need their SVGALIB V6.0. Which is available for free if you are not using it to make any money. (It comes in three parts)
          ftp://ftp.scitechsoft.com/devel/svbase60.exe
          ftp://ftp.scitechsoft.com/devel/svsrc60.exe
          ftp://ftp.scitechsoft.com/devel/svdem60.exe

      Another very nice piece of Software is PMODEW. A free DOS Extender (free as the above mentioned SVGALIB, non commercial only). I know of at least two homepages:
        http://www.dorsai.org/~daredevi/pmw/
        http://www.di.net/pmw
      At the first just mentioned page you can allways get the newest PMODEW version, right know it is PMODEW Version 1.24.
